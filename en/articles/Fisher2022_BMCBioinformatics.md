# The Xenopus phenotype ontology: bridging genotype to phenotype

## Citation (NLM)

## Background
- Phenotype descriptions in Xenopus developmental and disease model research are non-standardized, hindering cross-species comparison and data integration
- Phenotype ontology systematically links genotype-to-phenotype relationships, standardizes interpretation of high-throughput screening results
- Ontology integrable with model organism databases like Xenbase is needed
- Semantic annotation of large-scale phenotypic data from CRISPR screening and chemical genetics is required

## Key Experiment Methods
1. **Xenopus phenotype ontology development**: Systematization of stage-specific and organ-specific phenotype terms, mapping to mammalian phenotype ontology (MP)
2. **Xenbase integration**: Ontology-based phenotype annotation in Xenbase database, enhanced gene-phenotype association search functionality
3. **Cross-species comparison**: Cross-referencing Xenopus phenotypes with human disease phenotypes, zebrafish, and mouse phenotypes
4. **CRISPR screening application**: Ontology-based annotation of large-scale gene knockdown/knockout screening results, phenotypic profile comparison
5. **Optimization parameters**: ontology term granularity, mapping accuracy, annotation consistency, cross-species homology criteria

## Results
- Standardized phenotype ontology established for Xenopus developmental and disease models
- Xenbase integration improved gene-phenotype association search and data query functionality
- Cross-species phenotype comparison enabled functional prediction of Xenopus orthologs of human disease genes
- Ontology-based annotation of CRISPR screening results enabled high-throughput phenotypic classification
- Semantic integration of occurrence data supports meta-analysis and systems biology research

## Perspective
- Phenotype ontology is established as standard data infrastructure for Xenopus functional genomics
- Cross-species ontology mapping accelerates comparative developmental biology and disease modeling
- Integration with AI-based automated phenotype annotation completes high-throughput screening pipelines
- Integration with other model organism ontologies advances universal developmental phenotype ontology
- Xenopus phenotypic data contributes to clinical genomic variant interpretation
