# Phenomics-Based Quantification of CRISPR-Induced Mosaicism in Zebrafish

## Citation (NLM)
Watson CJ, Monstad-Rios AT, Bhimani RM, Gistelinck C, Willaert A, Coucke P, Hsu YH, Kwon RY. Phenomics-based quantification of CRISPR-induced mosaicism in zebrafish. Cell Syst. 2020;10:275-286. doi: 10.1016/j.cels.2020.02.007

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cels.2020.02.007](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cels.2020.02.007)

## Background
- CRISPR-edited G0 zebrafish exhibit mosaic phenotypes (spatial variation) that are challenging to detect and interpret
- Phenomics uses large-scale phenotyping to systematically infer genotype-to-phenotype relationships
- Quantification of clonal cluster size distribution and phenotypic patterns in CRISPR-induced mosaics is needed
- Validation of whether somatic G0 mutants recapitulate the biology of germline homozygous mutants is required

## Key Experiment Methods
1. **Imaging-based phenomics**: Quantitative phenotyping at multiple sites within the axial skeleton, high-resolution imaging
2. **Clonal cluster analysis**: Characterization of loss-of-function cell cluster size distribution from CRISPR editing, elucidation of clonal fragmentation/merger events
3. **Large-scale phenotypic screening**: Enhanced sensitivity for detecting somatic mutant populations, statistical framework for spatial phenotypic variation analysis
4. **G0 vs germline comparison**: Phenotypic convergence confirmed between somatic mutants (plod2, bmp1a) and homozygous germline mutants
5. **Optimization parameters**: imaging resolution, number of analysis sites, clonal cluster size thresholds, statistical model selection

## Results
- Clonal clusters from CRISPR editing follow a universal size distribution
- Distinct phenotypic patterns identified from mosaic gene loss
- Large-scale phenotyping increased sensitivity for detecting somatic mutant populations
- Somatic mutants for plod2 and bmp1a recapitulated human osteogenesis imperfecta disease models
- Somatic G0 mutants faithfully recapitulated inbred disease model biology (phenotypic convergence)
- Statistical framework established for spatial phenotypic variation analysis

## Perspective
- Phenomics-based CRISPR screening is evolving as a high-throughput functional genomics pipeline
- Clonal cluster analysis contributes to cell fate and migration studies during development
- G0 mosaic analysis serves as a preliminary validation tool before germline mutant generation
- Future multi-organ and multi-timepoint phenotypic analysis will elucidate mosaic dynamics
- Statistical frameworks will expand to other CRISPR screening models
