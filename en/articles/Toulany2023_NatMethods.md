# Uncovering developmental time and tempo using deep learning

## Citation (NLM)
Toulany N, Morales-Navarrete H, Čapek D, Grathwohl J, Ünalan M, Müller P. Uncovering developmental time and tempo using deep learning. Nat Methods. 2023;20:2000-2010. doi: 10.1038/s41592-023-02083-8

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1038/s41592-023-02083-8](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41592-023-02083-8)

---

## Background
- Animal development involves complex morphological changes from fertilized egg to adult, with interspecies differences in developmental tempo emerging as principal drivers of evolutionary novelty
- Conventional developmental staging relies on manual microscopic observation; idealized staging atlases fail to capture individual variation and smooth stage transitions
- Deep learning is needed for automated, unbiased calculation of similarity between embryo images to quantify developmental time and tempo

## Key Experiment Methods
1. **Deep learning-based similarity calculation**: Automated computation of similarity between embryo images across different timepoints, derivation of complex phenotypic fingerprints
2. **Developmental time and tempo quantification**: Quantitative determination of developmental time and tempo from inter-stage similarities, temperature-dependent developmental speed measurement
3. **Unsupervised staging atlas derivation**: Automatic generation of staging atlases for multiple species de novo in an unsupervised manner
4. **Individual embryo developmental progression tracking**: Detection of natural and induced changes in developmental progression, quantification at individual embryo level
5. **Optimization parameters**: CNN architecture, similarity metric, timestamp resolution, interspecies comparison criteria, unsupervised clustering algorithm

## Results
- Deep learning achieved high-accuracy automated embryo staging
- Successfully quantified temperature-dependent developmental tempo
- Detected developmental progression changes from natural occurrence and induced perturbations (signaling inhibition, etc.) at individual embryo level
- Derived staging atlases for multiple species de novo in unsupervised fashion
- Phenotypic fingerprints carried characteristic information about developmental time and tempo
- Established objective, standardized methodology for early embryogenesis analysis

## Perspective
- Deep learning-based developmental staging overcomes limitations of manual observation, providing standardized analysis
- Interspecies developmental tempo comparison contributes to evolutionary developmental biology (evo-devo) research
- Unsupervised staging atlas derivation improves accessibility of developmental research for non-model organisms
- Quantitative assessment of environmental factors (temperature, chemicals) on developmental speed becomes possible
- Future multi-species and multi-condition developmental comparisons will elucidate evolutionary morphogenesis mechanisms
