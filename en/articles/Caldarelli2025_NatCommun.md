# AI-based approach to dissect the variability of mouse stem cell-derived embryo models

## Citation (NLM)
Caldarelli P, Deininger L, Zhao S, Panda P, Yang C, Mikut R, Zernicka-Goetz M. AI-based approach to dissect the variability of mouse stem cell-derived embryo models. Nat Commun. 2025;16:1772. doi: 10.1038/s41467-025-56908-5

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-56908-5](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-56908-5)

## Background
- Stem cell-derived embryo models (ETiX-embryos) have transformed developmental biology, but variability in developmental outcomes is high due to limited understanding of early stages
- ETiX-embryos are constructed by aggregating ESCs with TSCs (derived from extra-embryonic ectoderm precursors), mimicking post-implantation development
- Lack of detailed characterization of initial cell and tissue self-organization phases hinders experimental standardization and limits high-throughput screening applications
- Deep learning is needed to improve reproducibility of embryo model selection and forecast developmental trajectories

## Key Experiment Methods
1. **Live imaging + AI classification model**: Live imaging of 900 mouse post-implantation ETiX-embryos, normal/abnormal classification
2. **Time-dependent prediction accuracy**: 88% accuracy at 90h post-cell seeding, 65% accuracy at initial cell-seeding stage for developmental trajectory forecasting
3. **Morphological feature analysis**: Normally developed ETiX-embryos exhibit higher cell counts, larger size, and more compact shape
4. **Perturbation experiments**: Increased initial cell numbers improved normal development outcomes, causal relationship validation
5. **Optimization parameters**: CNN architecture, imaging timepoints, cell count thresholds, morphological features (size, compactness), perturbation conditions

## Results
- Best-performing AI model achieved 88% accuracy at 90h and 65% at initial stage for normal/abnormal classification
- Normally developing ETiX-embryos characterized by higher cell counts, larger size, and compact shape
- Perturbation experiments (increased initial cell numbers) confirmed improvement in normal development outcomes
- Deep learning improved reproducibility of stem cell-derived embryo model selection
- Key features of ETiX-embryo self-organization elucidated

## Perspective
- AI-based embryo model selection contributes to standardization of stem cell-derived developmental models
- Early developmental stage prediction is applicable to embryo quality control and experimental design optimization
- Perturbation + AI integration accelerates elucidation of self-organization mechanisms
- Extension to human stem cell-derived embryo models will advance regenerative medicine and disease modeling
- High-throughput screening platform enables evaluation of drug and environmental factor effects on embryos
