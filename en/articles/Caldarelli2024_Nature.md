# AI-guided embryo model selection reveals principles of self-organization

## Citation (NLM)
Caldarelli P, et al. AI-guided embryo model selection reveals principles of self-organization. Nature. 2024;633:887-895. doi: 10.1038/s41586-024-07934-8

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-07934-8](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-07934-8)

## Background
- Stem cell-derived embryo models have revolutionized developmental mechanism research, but limited understanding of self-organization principles restricts reproducibility
- AI-based selection identifies normally developing models, elucidating key factors of self-organization
- Live imaging + deep learning captures dynamic processes of early development
- Mouse post-implantation models have translational potential for human development and disease research

## Key Experiment Methods
1. **AI-based embryo model selection**: Live imaging + CNN for normal/abnormal ETiX-embryo classification, developmental trajectory prediction
2. **Self-organization principle elucidation**: Quantification of cell number, spatial arrangement, and signaling pathway effects on self-organization
3. **Perturbation analysis**: Causal relationship validation of self-organization through initial condition modifications (cell number, media composition, inhibitor treatment)
4. **Cross-species comparison**: Comparison of mouse embryo models with human development, identification of conserved self-organization principles
5. **Optimization parameters**: CNN architecture, imaging timepoints, perturbation conditions, self-organization metric definition

## Results
- AI achieved high-precision selection of normally developing embryo models, early prediction of developmental trajectories
- Cell number and spatial arrangement identified as key factors for self-organization success
- Perturbation experiments validated causal relationships in self-organization
- Conserved self-organization principles identified between mouse and human
- AI-based selection improved reproducibility of stem cell-derived developmental models

## Perspective
- AI + stem cell-derived embryo models transform developmental biology research paradigms
- Self-organization principle elucidation contributes to regenerative medicine and organogenesis research
- AI-based quality control standardizes production of clinically applicable embryo models
- Human embryo model expansion accelerates disease modeling and drug discovery
- Understanding self-organization leads to synthetic morphology and artificial organ development
