# MicroCT-based phenomics in the zebrafish skeleton reveals virtues of deep phenotyping in a distributed organ system

## Citation (NLM)
Hur M, Gistelinck CA, Huber P, Lee J, Thompson MH, Monstad-Rios AT, Watson CJ, McMenamin SK, Willaert A, Parichy DM, Coucke P, Kwon RY. MicroCT-based phenomics in the zebrafish skeleton reveals virtues of deep phenotyping in a distributed organ system. eLife. 2017;6:e26014. doi: 10.7554/eLife.26014

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.7554/eLife.26014](https://doi.org/10.7554/eLife.26014)

## Background
- Advances in genomic sequencing have revolutionized our ability to identify gene variants, but our capacity to characterize vertebrate phenomes (in-depth phenotypic profiles at whole-organism scale) remains limited
- The skeleton is a spatially distributed organ system throughout the body, an ideal target for phenomic approaches
- Conventional skeletal phenotypic analysis is limited to a few sites; methods for simultaneous deep analysis of multiple bones/compartments are lacking
- High-throughput, high-dimensional phenotypic analysis platforms are needed to match the throughput of genomic screening

## Key Experiment Methods
1. **FishCuT supervised segmentation algorithm**: Automated segmentation of all vertebral bodies in the adult zebrafish axial skeleton, quantification of morphological and densitometric traits
2. **High-dimensional phenotypic profiling**: Extraction of hundreds of phenotypic measures (morphology, density) at multiple sites within the axial skeleton, pattern-based analysis compared to individual vertebra analysis
3. **Statistical phenomic pattern analysis**: Testing for phenomic pattern differences between mutant populations, allometric models to identify phenotypes masked by growth alterations
4. **Human brittle bone disease model validation**: Deep phenotyping of plod2-/- and bmp1a-/- mutants to identify phenomic signatures associated with human osteogenesis imperfecta
5. **opallus mutant analysis**: Phenotypic characterization of novel axial skeletal mutant associated with TSH receptor hyperactivity
6. **Optimization parameters**: microCT resolution, FishCuT segmentation thresholds, vertebral body definition criteria, allometric model variables, statistical testing methods

## Results
- FishCuT successfully automated segmentation and quantification of all vertebral bodies in adult zebrafish axial skeleton
- Vertebral pattern analysis improved sensitivity for discriminating mutant populations compared to individual vertebra analysis (maintaining specificity)
- Phenomic signatures associated with human brittle bone disease identified in plod2-/- and bmp1a-/- mutants
- Novel axial skeletal phenotypes associated with TSH receptor hyperactivity discovered in opallus mutants
- Allometric models enabled identification of mutant phenotypes masked by growth changes
- Hundreds of phenotypic measures extracted at high throughput from multiple sites, matching genomic screening throughput

## Perspective
- Deep phenotyping presents a new paradigm for analyzing spatially distributed organ systems
- Phenotypic pattern analysis enhances genetic screening productivity and enables study of small-effect-size genetic variants
- FishCuT + microCT platform is evolving as a standard tool for zebrafish skeletal genomics
- High-throughput vertebrate model phenotypic analysis contributes to functional interpretation of human disease-related genes
- Future expansion to multi-organ and multi-timepoint phenomics will advance systems biology research
