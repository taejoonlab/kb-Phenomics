# Label-free evaluation of mouse embryo quality using time-lapse bright field and optical coherence microscopy

## Background
- High-quality embryo selection is essential for improving implantation rates in IVF; conventional morphological evaluation is limited by static timepoints and subjective judgment
- Time-lapse imaging enables continuous monitoring but is mostly restricted to 2D images; 3D noninvasive imaging is needed
- Optical coherence microscopy (OCM) provides high-resolution, volumetric, noninvasive imaging capable of resolving cellular/subcellular features of embryos
- 4D live imaging of the entire embryonic development process (1-cell → fully hatched blastocyst) within an incubator is required

## Key Experiment Methods
1. **Time-lapse 3D OCM imaging**: Combined with co-registered bright-field imaging, continuous imaging of mouse embryo development全过程 within an incubator
2. **OCM-based structural feature detection**: Noninvasive detection of cellular-level microstructures in developing embryos, resolution of trophectoderm and zona pellucida
3. **Embryonic cell cycle analysis**: Association of second and third embryonic cell cycles with blastocyst formation and hatching capability
4. **Label-free evaluation**: Embryo quality assessment based solely on structural features without staining or genetic manipulation, potential for IVF clinical application
5. **Optimization parameters**: OCM resolution (numerical aperture), imaging interval (time-lapse), incubator environmental control, bright-field co-registration

## Results
- OCM demonstrated capability to detect structural features of developing embryos
- Second and third embryonic cell cycles were shown to be associated with blastocyst formation and hatching capability
- Time-lapse OCM holds potential to enrich insights into embryonic development and streamline embryo selection in IVF clinics
- Label-free, noninvasive imaging enables embryo quality evaluation, circumventing invasiveness limitations of PGT
- 4D live imaging captures dynamic events of early embryonic development

## Perspective
- OCM-based time-lapse imaging can evolve as a standard tool for IVF embryo selection
- Label-free evaluation improves quality prediction accuracy while maintaining embryo integrity
- 3D imaging + AI-based analysis enables automation and objectification of embryo selection
- Extension to human embryos will contribute to improved clinical IVF success rates
- Noninvasive imaging has broad applications as a platform for regenerative medicine and developmental biology research
