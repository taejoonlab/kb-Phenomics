# Deep learning-based high-resolution time inference for deciphering dynamic gene regulation from fixed embryos

## Citation (NLM)
Bao H, Zhang S, Yu Z, Xu H. Deep learning-based high-resolution time inference for deciphering dynamic gene regulation from fixed embryos. Nat Commun. 2025;16:6565. doi: 10.1038/s41467-025-61907-7

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-61907-7](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-61907-7)

## Background
- Embryo development is driven by spatiotemporal dynamics of complex gene regulatory networks; traditional live-imaging is limited in simultaneously tracking multiple molecular species (<4)
- Fixed-embryo imaging offers high sensitivity and spatial resolution but lacks temporal resolution
- Manual staging or morphology atlas comparison provides only coarse-grained stages, inadequate for molecular-level dynamics interpretation
- Deep learning is needed for high-precision inference of absolute developmental time from nuclear morphology in fixed embryo images

## Key Experiment Methods
1. **Multi-scale ensemble deep learning**: Absolute developmental time inferred at 1-minute resolution from nuclear morphology in Drosophila fixed embryo images
2. **Fixed-embryo quantitative imaging**: Wild-type embryo fixation and imaging to decipher spatiotemporal regulation of segmentation gene Krüppel (Kr) by multiple transcription factors (TFs)
3. **Time-resolved theoretical model**: Integration with single-molecule mRNA statistics model, elucidation of unsteady-state bursty kinetics of endogenous segmentation gene hunchback (hb) driven by dynamic TF binding
4. **Dynamics interpretation without genetic modification**: Endogenous gene regulation dynamics deciphered without genetic modification
5. **Optimization parameters**: multi-scale CNN architecture, nuclear morphology features, time bin size, ensemble composition, mRNA burst kinetics model parameters

## Results
- Deep learning accurately inferred absolute developmental time at 1-minute resolution from fixed embryo images
- Spatiotemporal regulation of segmentation gene Krüppel (Kr) by multiple TFs deciphered
- Unsteady-state bursty kinetics of hunchback (hb) gene revealed to be driven by dynamic TF binding
- Versatile framework provided for deciphering complex gene network dynamics without genetic modification
- Deep learning overcame temporal resolution limitations of fixed-embryo imaging

## Perspective
- Deep learning-based time inference is evolving as a standard analytical tool in developmental genomics
- Fixed-embryo imaging + AI circumvents live-imaging limitations, enabling simultaneous multi-molecular species analysis
- Time-resolved models contribute to understanding gene expression burst kinetics
- Expandable to other model organisms (Drosophila → zebrafish, Xenopus, mammalian)
- Contributes to comprehensive mapping of gene regulatory networks during development
